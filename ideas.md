# Coachella 2026 Website Design Ideas

## Approach 1: Desert Noir
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Neo-Brutalist Desert Noir
**Core Principles:** Raw energy, high contrast, editorial boldness, tactile texture
**Color Philosophy:** Deep midnight black (#0A0A0A) with electric sand gold (#F5C842) and dusty terracotta (#C4623A). The palette evokes the Coachella desert at night — scorched earth meets neon festival lights.
**Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric editorial grid. Artist cards break the grid intentionally. Day tabs as oversized typographic anchors. Video embeds float with offset shadows.
**Signature Elements:** Grain/noise texture overlay on all sections; oversized stencil-style day labels (FRIDAY / SATURDAY / SUNDAY); diagonal cut dividers between sections
**Interaction Philosophy:** Hover states reveal artist info with a slide-up reveal. Clicking a card expands it inline without navigation.
**Animation:** Staggered entrance animations on scroll. Cards slide in from alternating sides. Day headers stamp in with a scale+opacity animation.
**Typography System:** "Bebas Neue" for display headings (all-caps, condensed); "DM Sans" for body text. Hierarchy: 96px display → 48px section → 24px card title → 16px body
</text>

## Approach 2: Psychedelic Modernism
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** 70s Psychedelic Revival meets Swiss Modernism
**Core Principles:** Vibrant chromatic energy, structured chaos, retro-futurism, immersive color fields
**Color Philosophy:** Deep indigo (#1A0A3E) background with acid lime (#C8F135), hot coral (#FF5C4D), and electric violet (#8B5CF6). Colors shift in gradient zones between sections — each day has its own chromatic identity.
**Layout Paradigm:** Horizontal scroll sections per day with a fixed vertical timeline on the left. Artist cards use polaroid-style frames with rotated angles.
**Signature Elements:** Wavy SVG dividers between sections; retro halftone dot patterns; glowing neon text shadows on headliner names
**Interaction Philosophy:** Parallax scroll creates depth. Cards tilt on hover (CSS perspective transform). Video modal opens with a radial expand animation.
**Animation:** Continuous slow gradient shift on hero background. Scroll-triggered wave animations on section dividers. Headliner names have a chromatic aberration flicker on load.
**Typography System:** "Syne" (bold, geometric) for headings; "Space Grotesk" for body. Headliner names get a custom gradient text treatment.
</text>

## Approach 3: Cinematic Desert Dusk — CHOSEN
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Cinematic Desert Dusk — warm, immersive, editorial
**Core Principles:** Warmth and depth, cinematic atmosphere, editorial clarity, festival energy
**Color Philosophy:** Rich warm charcoal (#1C1410) as the base, with sunset amber (#E8923A), dusty rose (#D4748C), and cool sage (#7FB5A0) as accent triads. The palette captures the Coachella golden hour — that magical moment when the sun sets over the desert and the stages light up.
**Layout Paradigm:** Full-bleed hero with a sticky navigation bar. Day sections use a magazine-style masonry layout for artist cards. The Weekend 2 section uses a horizontal card scroll. No centered grid — everything is left-aligned with intentional breathing room.
**Signature Elements:** Warm gradient overlays on images; thin horizontal rule separators with dot accents; artist cards with a bottom-anchored info strip (like film credits)
**Interaction Philosophy:** Cards reveal video thumbnails on hover with a warm overlay lift. Clicking opens a YouTube embed modal. Day filter tabs use an underline-slide indicator.
**Animation:** Hero has a slow Ken Burns effect on the background. Cards entrance with a staggered fade-up. Modal opens with a smooth scale-from-center. Scroll-triggered section reveals.
**Typography System:** "Playfair Display" for headliner names (elegant, editorial); "Outfit" for all other text (clean, modern, versatile). Hierarchy: 80px display → 42px section → 20px card title → 15px body
</text>

</response>

## Selected Approach: Cinematic Desert Dusk

Chosen for its balance of warmth, editorial sophistication, and festival energy. The warm charcoal base with sunset amber accents perfectly captures the Coachella aesthetic without resorting to clichéd purple gradients or generic festival design tropes.
